Robots in our Society

What do Robots do in our society?

If you're visitng Autonopedia, you may already know what a robot is. You also may not! Robots are commonly misconstrued in definition, and it vaies from person to person. One may think that robots are all autonomous, and might take over the world one day! However, the informed reader of the Autonopedia website will know that not all robots are autonomous. Robots are simply computers like any other, that can be programmed to do what we ask. Mostly, robots just do our menial labour, like on the automotive production lines. However, robots can be much greater than that! Robots can be found in the medical field, being operated by skilled surgeons saving lives. Some robots also scan, process and check out your groceries when you go shopping. Robots are even found in childrens toys, like robot pets! Just remembe, when you think "What is a robot?", think "Endless possibilities!".

Brief overview of Autonomy

Autonomy in robots has been a thoroughly researched area of robotics. W. Grey Walter, commonly hailed as the father of Autonomy, had first discovered this concept with Elmer and Elsie, in 1948. Those robots helped pave the way for generations worth of work in robotics and autonomy. In today's day and age, Autonomous robots can be found everywhere you look! From self-driving delivery robots to robots that work for us in space, there is no shortage of amazing robots around (or above) our world!
