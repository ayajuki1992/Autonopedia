Robots in our Society

Famous robots from around the world

You may already be aware of some quite famous autonomous robots from around the world. Just to the left you can see one of the most famous robots from our time. NASA's Mars rover, named Opportunity, was only meant to operate for 90 sols (92.5 Earth days) but actually persevered (Pun intended) for a whopping 15 Earth years! Sadly, a large, planet wide dust storm had cut off the rover's ability to generate power from its solar panels. Despite that fact, it still managed 26.219 miles, and managed to figure out that there was once water on Mars! It's simply incredible what one rover could achieve on its own, and report back to home base. Below, you can find another example of some very famous autonomous robots. Brief overview of LLMs (Large Language Models)

Whether you believe that AI counts as an autonomous robot, is up to you. Large Language Models like ChatGPT and Google Bard aren't as new a concept as you may imagine. One of the first LLMs was developed around 1967. The program was made by Weizenbaum, and he named it ELIZA. ChatGPT and Google Bard fall quite similar to their roots, where you can talk to it, and it can reply in a natural sounding manner. People tend to use these tools to assist in their work, generate ideas or simply just talk to them for fun. You can even ask them to make you a song! ⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀ (Although, not very good)
